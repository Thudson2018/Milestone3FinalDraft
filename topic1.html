Topic 1: History of the Browsers

Beginning in Geneva, Switzerland in the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN.) Tim Berners-Lee created the first Web server and browser and was initially called the “World Wide Web” later being called “Nexus.” This marked the beginning for future companies to create their own browsers, later giving us open-source browsers in 2004 with the release of FireFox by Modzilla. This sparked the competition to create other more polished open source browsers such as Google releasing “Chromium” now known as “Chrome” that was praised for its ease of use and faster speeds. But with Apple running on another operating system called “MacOS” and “IOS” led to them creating their own browser called “Safari in 2003” optimized for their own unique operating systems.
